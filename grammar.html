Tibetan Grammar Terms

Quick reference guide for core linguistic and structural definitions.

Syllable
The fundamental phonetic and written unit in Tibetan script, typically centered around a root letter with optional vowels, prefixes, and suffixes, separated by a tsek (་).
ཚིག་བརྩེགས
Vowel
Dependent vowel signs (dbyangs) written above or below the root letter to modify its inherent 'a' sound (e.g., i, u, e, o).
དབྱངས
Head Letter
Superfixed letters (mgo yig) that sit vertically stacked on top of certain root letters (e.g., ra, la, sa).
མགོ་ཅན
Prefix
Letters attached to the left side of a root letter (sngon 'jug) that influence the tonal category and pronunciation of the syllable.
སྔོན་འཇུག
Suffix
Letters attached to the right side of the root stack (rjes 'jug) that close the syllable.
རྗེས་འཇུག
Noun
A word representing a person, place, thing, or idea, which takes specific grammatical case particles in Tibetan.
མིང་ཚིག
Verb
An action or state of being word, which typically anchors the end of a Tibetan clause or sentence and inflects across different time tenses.
བྱ་ཚིག
